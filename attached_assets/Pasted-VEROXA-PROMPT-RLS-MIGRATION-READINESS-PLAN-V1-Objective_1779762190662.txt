PART 2 — ROLE ACCESS RULES

Use these role rules:

CLIENT

Can view:
- their own client record
- their own platform connection status
- their own onboarding items
- their own media assets, but not internal rejection notes unless client-safe
- their own post calendar
- their own notifications
- their own weekly reports
- their own monthly reports
- their own requests

Can create:
- media assets for their own client account
- client requests
- maybe onboarding item updates for client-owned items

Cannot view:
- internal draft variants
- internal team notes
- other clients
- owner revenue
- operator risk comments
- activity logs not meant for them
- internal AI agent reasoning

TEAM

Can view:
- assigned clients
- assigned media assets
- concepts/drafts/posts for assigned clients
- weekly reports for assigned clients
- relevant notifications and work queues

Can create/update:
- media review statuses
- content concepts
- draft sets
- draft variants
- posts before publishing
- weekly report drafts
- activity logs through system/app action

Cannot:
- change pricing
- change user roles
- approve monthly reports as final operator approval
- view owner revenue dashboard unless explicitly allowed

OPERATOR

Can view:
- all clients
- all client health
- all reports
- all alerts
- all media oversight
- all team workload

Can update:
- risk status
- monthly report approval status
- escalation/dismissal of alerts
- client health review notes
- team oversight statuses

Cannot:
- change pricing
- change owner permissions
- delete core records casually
- become required approval gate for every post

OWNER

Can view:
- everything

Can update:
- client account status
- pricing/package fields
- role permissions
- internal user access
- business-level settings

Should be the only role allowed to change:
- clients.plan_type
- clients.service_package
- clients.monthly_fee_cents
- user_profiles.role
- account_status if closing/pausing client

SYSTEM / AUTOMATION

Can insert/update:
- activity_logs
- notifications
- derived health snapshots
- report aggregation updates
- post publish status after publishing
- media used status after post publication

System actions should always create activity_logs.
