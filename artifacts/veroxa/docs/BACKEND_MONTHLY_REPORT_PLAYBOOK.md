# Backend Monthly Report Playbook

Monthly reports are deeper proof/reporting summaries, but they must stay honest, client-safe, and manual until live systems are approved.

## Preparation checklist

- Summarize what Veroxa handled during the month.
- Review Google/Maps/local visibility upkeep and website/menu/contact alignment work.
- Review Facebook/Instagram picture-based content support and media supply.
- Include only available reach/action signals with attribution confidence and limitations.
- Identify what is needed next: media, confirmations, access, or client decisions.
- Send the report through Faraz/team review before client delivery.

## Example monthly report structure

1. Month and restaurant name.
2. What Veroxa handled.
3. Google/Maps/local visibility progress.
4. Website/menu/contact alignment progress.
5. Facebook/Instagram content progress.
6. Media used and media needed next.
7. Reach/action signal wording with limitations.
8. Next month focus.

## Reach/action signal wording

- “Available online presence signals were reviewed directionally.”
- “Calls, directions, menu visits, order-link activity, or owner-reported customer mentions should be treated as signals, not guaranteed new sales.”
- “Attribution confidence should be stated as confirmed, strong signal, directional, owner reported, or unknown when enough context exists.”

## Limitations wording

- “This report does not claim generated sales.”
- “No fake metrics should be added when live analytics are unavailable.”
- “Internal proof math stays internal and must not appear in the client report.”

## Hard boundaries

- No fake metrics.
- No proof math.
- No generated-sales claims.
- No guarantees for orders, revenue, rankings, ROI, walk-ins, profit, customers, or growth.
- No live systems or live publishing claims.
- Faraz/team review is required before delivery.
