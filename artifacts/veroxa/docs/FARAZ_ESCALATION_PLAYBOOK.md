# Faraz Escalation Playbook

Escalate when a request needs owner judgment, business-truth confirmation, sensitive handling, or scope protection.

## When to escalate

- Sensitive or risky requests: health/legal claims, religious/dietary claims, serious complaints, aggressive wording, or public controversy.
- Business-truth ambiguity: hours, holiday hours, menu changes, prices, offers, catering, availability, access, or ownership details.
- Low client cooperation: repeated missing media, missing confirmations, unclear instructions, or unavailable access.
- Out-of-scope pressure: routine calls/texts, customer-service handling, full custom website work, ads, Yelp/TikTok/Reels, daily posting, live integrations, or guaranteed results.
- Value/proof concern: unclear online-influenced signals, weak media supply, client confusion about expectations, or internal value confidence concerns.
- First-client discount confusion: questions about 20% off first 12 months and continuously active eligibility.

## What to include

- Restaurant name and portal request/media/report context.
- Exact client wording.
- What Veroxa can safely do.
- What requires confirmation.
- Which boundary applies: included, add-on, coming soon, not included, or risky.
- Recommended next response.

## Escalation note examples

- “Faraz review needed: client asks to advertise a new discount, but we do not have confirmed offer dates or restrictions. Recommend asking for exact terms before drafting.”
- “Faraz review needed: client wants us to reply to a serious complaint. Customer-service replies are not included; recommend telling the restaurant to respond directly.”
- “Faraz review needed: low media supply for two weeks. Recommend portal reminder and weekly update limitation wording.”
- “Faraz review needed: client expects guaranteed orders from monthly service. Recommend restating no guarantees and explaining online presence signal review.”

## Handling rules

- Do not publish, message customers, change platforms, or promise outcomes while waiting for Faraz.
- Hold risky items until confirmation required items are resolved.
- Keep notes calm, specific, and action-focused.
