# Momo Founding Pilot Commitment and Onboarding Gate

Status: current governing direction as of 2026-07-13.

## Relationship and commercial commitment

Momo's House San Antonio has already agreed to onboard with Veroxa as the founding pilot restaurant.

Momo understands that Veroxa is still being built as a product and operating platform. Faraz has told Momo that he will contact them when Veroxa is ready.

Momo is not a sales prospect waiting for a pitch and does not need to be re-sold on onboarding. The remaining question is when Veroxa is operationally ready to begin the agreed onboarding.

Faraz will not charge Momo during the remaining build phase or the initial founding-pilot operating period. Charging Momo begins only when Veroxa is mature, dependable, and proven enough that Faraz is ready to expand and market Veroxa to additional restaurants. No subscription, invoice, checkout, payment method, or charge may be created for Momo before that business milestone and a separate explicit Faraz approval.

Momo must be treated as a founding restaurant partner, not as an uninformed beta tester. Veroxa may learn from the pilot, but the first active experience must be secure, truthful, polished, and useful.

## Current build direction

Stop adding disconnected planning, checklist, or dashboard surfaces unless they directly enable or clarify an operational workflow.

The product direction is to complete and prove the real Momo operating loop using the existing GitHub, Supabase, Codex, ChatGPT, and Sites stack:

1. Preserve GitHub `main` as the canonical product and operating source of truth.
2. Keep ChatGPT as Faraz's primary command center and Codex as the implementation workflow invoked through ChatGPT.
3. Keep ChatGPT Sites as the sole deployment surface; Vercel remains retired.
4. Keep Supabase as the sole authentication, Postgres, and private-storage backend.
5. Maintain exact GitHub/Sites source parity for every authorized deployment.
6. Keep Momo as the only operational restaurant until the founding pilot succeeds.
7. Keep other restaurants inside Restaurant Audit Center as audit records or explicitly consented pending, non-operational profiles only.
8. Finish persistent Momo onboarding, owner-confirmed restaurant truth, contacts, presence, access requirements, and client training.
9. Finish the private media and rights workflow, including consent, Team review, quality status, tags, and safe reuse.
10. Prove persistent messages, requests, corrections, work items, approvals, activity, evidence, reports, monitoring, retries, recovery, and backups.
11. Prove the manual content loop before activating paid AI or external publishing:

   `permissioned media -> truthful draft -> Team/Faraz review -> schedule -> manual publication record -> client status -> activity evidence -> weekly/monthly report`

12. Use deterministic/manual drafting first. Runtime AI may be connected only after the manual loop is dependable, the review gate is proven, secrets stay server-side, costs are approved, and no customer-visible output bypasses human control.
13. Keep Meta, Google, publishing, and other owner-controlled integrations disconnected until onboarding establishes owner authority and Faraz explicitly approves the specific connection.
14. Continue the free-first rule: build production-quality foundations with current resources; do not introduce a new paid service or usage commitment without Faraz's explicit approval.

## When to tell Momo Veroxa is ready

Do not use a calendar estimate as the readiness decision. Tell Momo that onboarding is ready only when the evidence-based Momo onboarding gate below passes.

### Required gate

All of the following must be true:

- A separate approved Momo Client identity can be provisioned securely without public signup.
- Team and Client accounts are distinct and tested.
- Restaurant membership and RLS isolation are proven; a Client cannot access another restaurant and cannot access Team-only data.
- The deployed Sites source exactly matches the reviewed and merged GitHub source.
- Momo client routes show safe empty or pending states and contain no unsupported completed, reviewed, sent, published, or performance claims.
- The complete Momo onboarding flow persists to Supabase and can be resumed without data loss.
- Business truth clearly distinguishes owner-confirmed, pending confirmation, optional, and internal-review fields.
- Private media upload, rights attestation, consent, Team review, and restaurant-scoped retrieval work on supported mobile and desktop browsers.
- Persistent client messages and structured requests work across separate devices.
- Team work items, assignments, blockers, approvals, and activity evidence persist correctly.
- At least one complete internal rehearsal succeeds from onboarding through media, draft, approval, manual publication record, activity evidence, and weekly report.
- Reports are created only from real recorded activity and contain no invented metrics, ROI, ranking, reach, orders, customers, or growth claims.
- Monitoring, retry, recovery, backup, and rollback procedures are tested for the pilot-critical path.
- Mobile/browser QA passes for the exact Team and Client journeys Momo will use.
- No critical or high-severity security or data-integrity defect remains open.
- The readiness system records an evidence-backed Go for onboarding. No synthetic percentage substitutes for the gate.
- Faraz explicitly approves contacting Momo and starting the onboarding appointment.

### Onboarding-ready is not full automation-ready

Momo may begin the agreed onboarding once the secure persistent manual operating loop and the required gate above pass. Live AI, Meta, Google, and automated publishing do not all need to be active before onboarding, provided their unavailable states are honest and the first pilot can be operated safely through reviewed manual execution.

The pilot should initially use human-controlled publication and platform work. Paid AI and live integrations may be activated later in modules after owner authorization, cost approval, security review, and successful manual-pilot evidence.

## Pilot operating and charging milestone

After onboarding, Veroxa should operate Momo through real weekly cycles, repair issues discovered through actual use, and collect truthful evidence of delivery and outcomes.

Momo remains free during the founding-pilot period. A future charging decision requires all of the following:

- the Momo operating loop has been dependable for multiple real cycles;
- service delivery can be repeated for another restaurant without rebuilding the platform;
- reporting and attribution language is honest and supportable;
- security, support, monitoring, and recovery are dependable;
- Faraz is ready to market Veroxa to additional restaurants;
- Faraz separately approves the commercial transition and communicates it to Momo.

Do not silently apply public pricing, a loyalty discount, a trial expiration, or a retroactive balance to Momo. The founding-pilot commercial arrangement overrides generic pricing copy until Faraz explicitly changes it.

## Alignment contract

This document governs ChatGPT planning, Codex implementation, GitHub source-of-truth updates, Supabase work, Sites application behavior, deployment handoffs, and readiness recommendations.

Every future Veroxa build must report two separate states:

- **Veroxa delivery state:** source, tests, migrations, security, deployment, and parity.
- **Momo onboarding readiness:** identity, truth, media, workflow, rehearsal, QA, recovery, and explicit Faraz approval.

A green PR, successful deployment, or completed foundation does not by itself authorize contacting Momo. Conversely, onboarding does not require every future paid integration to be active when the secure manual pilot loop is fully usable and the unavailable capabilities are represented honestly.
