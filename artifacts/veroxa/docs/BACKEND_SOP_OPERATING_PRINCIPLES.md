# Backend SOP Operating Principles

## Purpose

This document defines how a future Pakistan backend team should support Veroxa without expanding Team Portal complexity or adding live systems prematurely.

## Principles

- **Portal-first:** routine work starts and stays in the portal. No routine calls/texts for normal service requests.
- **24-hour response:** response means answer, review, or next step within 24 hours; it is not guaranteed completion.
- **No guarantees:** never promise rankings, revenue, orders, profit, ROI, customers, walk-ins, or growth.
- **No offer invention:** do not create discounts, BOGO offers, lower prices, price cuts, or new promotions.
- **Confirmation required:** hours, holiday hours, address, phone, menu, prices if mentioned, existing offers, catering, dietary/health claims, order links, and reservation links must be confirmed before public copy.
- **Media quality standards:** prioritize clear food photos, best-seller photos, storefront/menu/contact usefulness, and avoid blurry/dark/repeated media.
- **Website alignment scope:** align name/address/phone, hours, menu/order/contact links, social links, simple descriptions, and basic local wording if access is provided.
- **Add-ons:** new basic website +$95 and missing Facebook/Instagram profile +$45/profile require manual scope review; no checkout is connected.
- **Coming soon:** Yelp, TikTok, Reels/video, ads, daily posting, automated publishing, and live integrations are not included at launch.
- **Escalate to Faraz:** unclear requests, sensitive complaints, risky claims, business-truth ambiguity, add-on uncertainty, or anything outside scope.
- **Pakistan backend factory model:** operators follow SOPs, document work, prepare drafts, and escalate; Faraz approves customer-visible action.
- **Quality control:** every prepared public action needs review for accuracy, tone, boundaries, and confirmation status.

## No live systems

No live auth, database, storage upload, AI call, publishing connector, platform integration, payment, webhook, cron job, or automated customer-visible execution is part of this SOP.
